Information sheet

You are invited to take part in a research study. Please read the description carefully. If you are happy to participate, click the 'Continue' button at the bottom of the page.

What is the purpose of this study?

This study will help us understand how people make decisions and learn when interacting with unfamiliar environments. Improving our understanding of these processes could have implications for education, health, and everyday decision-making.

What is involved in the study?

To start, we will explain a decision-making task and ask a few comprehension questions so that we can be sure you understand the task before you begin. You will then complete several blocks of the task, after which you will complete some short cognitive tasks. The whole study should take approximately 35 minutes.

Is there a performance related bonus associated with this study?

Yes. Please see the Prolific advertisement for details. The more points you earn, the higher the bonus. We aim to credit any performance related bonus via Prolific within one working week.

What if I don't want to take part?

Your participation is completely voluntary, so it is up to you whether you would like to take part. You can withdraw from the study at any point without having to provide any explanation. After the study is complete, you can withdraw your data for up to four weeks by contacting the research team (contact details below). After four weeks, data will be fully anonymised and it will no longer be possible to identify your individual data.

Will my taking part in the study be kept confidential?

Yes, your responses and data will be treated as strictly confidential. Giving basic demographic information (e.g., age, first language) at the end of the study is optional, and we request only your Prolific ID and digital initials as part of the consent process. If you are conducting this study via Prolific, or via a similar platform which holds a record of participant IP addresses, note that your IP address will not be transferred and stored outside of Prolific, for instance it will not be stored in the dataset used for analysis.

What will happen to my data?

The Data Protection Act 2018 and UK General Data Protection Regulations allow you control over your personal data and how it is used. Under GDPR, Bangor University is the data controller in relation to your personal data and will process your data for the purposes of this study. Digital research data will be stored on a password-protected computer. At the end of the study, the anonymised dataset may be deposited on a publicly available data repository so that other scientists can examine our results.

Who is running the study?

This study is led by Dr Sam Jones of Bangor University's Department of Psychology. The study has been given a favourable opinion by a Bangor University Research Ethics Committee.

Who do I contact if I have concerns?

If you have a concern about any aspect of this study, please contact Dr Sam Jones via email at samuel.jones@bangor.ac.uk. Alternatively, you might contact Bangor University's Senior Research Governance & Policy Officer directly at seniorresearchgovofficer@bangor.ac.uk.

Task instructions

You will see a branching network with a starting location (blue circle) and 16 other locations for you to search.

Each location hides a reward value. To reveal a value, click on it. Importantly, note that each click costs you -1 point.

You can only click locations next to already-revealed locations. These are highlighted in orange.

Once you have revealed the values of some locations, press the 'Execute plan' button and then use your keyboard's arrow keys to move around and collect the reward values. Note that you can only move to revealed locations and can only move in one direction (you can't go back on yourself).

Note also that you don't have to navigate to the end of a path. Just press spacebar when navigating if you would like to stop at your current location.

Your score equals the sum of rewards along the path you took minus your click costs. Again, you do not need to search every location.

Try to maximise your overall score. The more points you earn, the higher your performance related bonus.

Practice trials

You will now do two practice trials. These do not count toward your score.
